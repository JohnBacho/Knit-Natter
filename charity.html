Giving Back One Stitch at a Time

At Knit & Natter, we truly feel that knitting is more than a craft—it’s about disseminating warmth, comfort, and kindness in every form possible. It’s also a charity that connects enthusiastic knitters with causes that make a real difference, such as handmade donations to shelters, newborns, cancer patients, and any community in crisis.

Warm Hearts Winter Drive

This past winter, the community came out for the Warm Hearts Winter Drive-a drive to provide hand-knitted scarves, hats, and gloves to help battle the bitter cold. Our generous knitters provided more than 500 pairs of handmade winter essentials to homeless shelters and community outreach programs.

Each stitch was a degree of warmth, comfort, and kindness; sometimes, the most modest acts, like knitting, can be the deepest in meaning. Let us extend our most sincere appreciation to each of the participants!

Tiny Hats, Big Impact

Our wonderfully active community brought more than 1,000 newborn hats into the hospitals and neonatal care facilities in all states through the Tiny Hats, Big Impact project. These supple, well-knit pieces of headgear keep baby preemies, as well as newborns, warm and reduce possibilities of hypothermia, making them enter their life as healthy as possible.

It would be your time, effort, and generosity which have concretely changed many families’ lifestyles with great needs. Thus, this venture once more proved the fact that it is in little things-that count, one small hat at a time.
