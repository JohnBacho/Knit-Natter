Pattern

Garter Stitch

By Nancy Mills

Simple classic, good for beginners: The garter stitch is created simply by knitting every row. Fabric produced will be squishy, textured, and perfect for everything from hats to blankets.

Seed Stitch

By Bob Mills

Seed Stitch is a pretty, textured stitch pattern of knit and purl stitches that alternate to form a fabric with a bumpy look on both sides. Suitable for scarves, blankets, or adding subtle texture to any design.

Difficulty

Ribbing Stitch

By Sarah Jacobs

Ribbing is the most common stitch that involves knitting and purling in one row. This stitch forms a very stretchy, elastic fabric that is perfect to be used with cuffs, and collars.

Difficulty

Stockinette Stitch

By Linda Harris

The Stockinette Stitch is a smooth, elastic stitch pattern created by knitting one row on the front and purling one row on the back. The stockinette stitch is the basis for most garment designs from scarves to sweaters

Difficulty

Cable Stitch

By Jennifer Clark

The Cable Stitch makes for an elaborate pattern by crossing groups over each other, thereby creating a braided and twisted effect. Ideal for adding depth and interest to your cozy blankets to stylish sweaters.

Difficulty

Basketweave

By Emily Green

The Basketweave Stitch imitates a woven basket. In this pattern, blocks of knit stitches and purl stitches are used in an alternate manner. This is great for a textured scarf, throw, or even home decor.

Difficulty

Lace Stitch

By Maria Johnson

Lace Stitch involves knitting and yarn-overs to create delicate open patterns. It is a great technique to add elegance and airiness to shawls, scarves, and other delicate garments.

Difficulty

Fair Isle

By Rachel Adams

Fair Isle knitting has used up to five colors at a time in one row to form beautiful patterns. Commonly used for the creation of sweaters, hats, and mittens, this stitch gives way to bright, eye-catching design.
