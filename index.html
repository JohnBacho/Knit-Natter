Discover

Garter Stitch

By Nancy Mills

Simple classic, good for beginners: The garter stitch is created simply by knitting every row. Fabric produced will be squishy, textured, and perfect for everything from hats to blankets.

Difficulty

Seed Stitch

By Bob Mills

Seed Stitch is a pretty, textured stitch pattern of knit and purl stitches that alternate to form a fabric with a bumpy look on both sides. Suitable for scarves, blankets, or adding subtle texture to any design.

Difficulty

Rib Stitch

By Sarah Jacobs

Ribbing is the most common stitch that involves knitting and purling in one row. This stitch forms a very stretchy, elastic fabric that is perfect to be used with cuffs, and collars.

Difficulty

Stockinette Stitch

By Linda Harris

The Stockinette Stitch is a smooth, elastic stitch pattern created by knitting one row on the front and purling one row on the back. The stockinette stitch is the basis for most garment designs from scarves to sweaters

Difficulty

Cable Stitch

By Jennifer Clark

The Cable Stitch makes for an elaborate pattern by crossing groups over each other, thereby creating a braided and twisted effect. Ideal for adding depth and interest to your cozy blankets to stylish sweaters.

Difficulty

Basketweave

By Emily Green

The Basketweave Stitch imitates a woven basket. In this pattern, blocks of knit stitches and purl stitches are used in an alternate manner. This is great for a textured scarf, throw, or even home decor.

Difficulty

Cozy Knit Blanket

By Olivia Brooks

Warm and cozy to wrap yourself in on cold nights. This pattern, in a mix of garter stitch and chunky yarn, meant for knitters who seek classic elements to create something soft and comforting with a modern touch.

Floral Lace Throw

By Charlotte Harper

Delicate and elegant this throw features intricate lace patterns that form a floral design. Perfect for advanced knitters, this project brings both beauty and romance to any living room or bedroom.

Chunky Ribbed Blanket

By Emily Grant

This thick, ribbed blanket pattern is perfect for creating the ultimate winter accessory. Due to the chunky yarn and simple knit/purl texture, it’s fast and satisfying to make-a great way to keep warm.

Chevron Stripes Blanket

This is a bright-colored, striking blanket with a classic chevron pattern. It’s ideal for adding personality into your home decor and an ideal pattern for knitters who wish to experiment with color and texture.

This creatively put-together patchwork blanket blends a variety of textures and colors. Great for using up scrap yarn, this pattern allows room for creativity but still yields a beautiful finished product.

About Us

Knit & Natter – Threads That Bind

At Knit & Natter, we believe knitting is more than just a craft-it’s a way to connect, create, and inspire. Whether you’re picking up needles for the first time or you’ve been knitting for years, our platform is built to bring people together through shared creativity, support, and learning. Our mission is simple: to make knitting easy, fun, and social. We provide well-tutorial zed step-by-step instructions, inspiring patterns and a cozy place to hang out with other knitters no matter what their level of knightliness.

It’s More Than Knitting

Knit & Natter is more than a website-it’s a meeting place. We host virtual knitting events, events in person, and casual meetups over coffee. We offer a place where creativity meets conversation. Whether it be learning new techniques, tips, or unwinding with other knitters, we make every stitch part of a larger story.

Giving Back, One Stitch at a Time

We believe knitting can make a difference. Through our charity initiatives, members can create handmade blankets, hats, and scarves for those in need. Every project brings warmth and comfort to individuals and communities, proving that something as simple as a stitch can have a profound impact.

Join Us

Whatever you’re a Beginner or Expert come one, come all. Whether it’s Learn, Socialize and Give, or whatever-the reasons, this is your safe place, welcomed with open arms and plenty of yarn. Knit and Natter your way into making a difference.
